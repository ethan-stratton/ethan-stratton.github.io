# Seventh Son — Game Design Document

_A modernized Link to the Past formula. 2D top-down (3/4 perspective). High fantasy._
_Tone: children's game with adult themes — Dark Crystal, Iron Giant, Coraline, Fantastic Mr. Fox._
_Vibe: Legend of Zelda × Shakespeare × Mythology. Fantastical but grounded._
_Core lesson: adults make mistakes and must learn from them._

---

## Core Concept

You are the **seventh son of the seventh son** — the first to ever exist. Born mute, with glowing magical eyes, in a world where scholars theorized this was possible but nobody was reckless enough to engineer it. Your grandfather, the king, was. Your father fled the prophecy, changed his name, and raised you as a farmer's child. You are 10-12 years old when soldiers come to your home, take your mother, and leave you alone.

---

## Story

### The Prophecy
- The number seven is woven into this world's fabric (seven notes, seven colors, seven days)
- Scholars theorized centuries ago that a seventh son of a seventh son would be born with innate magic
- It was academic speculation — predicted but never observed. Like theoretical physics
- Your grandfather was the first person insane enough to engineer the experiment. He fathered seven sons specifically for this purpose
- **You are the first seventh son of the seventh son to ever exist.** There is no precedent
- Nobody knows exactly what your powers are or what they'll become — you're proof of concept
- The grandfather wants your eyes not just for power but to STUDY the magic, understand it, replicate it. You're a specimen

### The King (Grandfather) — ALIVE, Main Antagonist
- A good king. Beloved by his people. Effective ruler. This is what makes it hard
- He studied history and learned that magical rule brings centuries of stability
- His kingdom is weakening — neighbors growing stronger, succession crises looming
- He diagnosed the problem correctly: the kingdom needs magical bloodline to survive
- His methods are monstrous but his reasoning is sound. He might even be RIGHT
- His ego: he believes he's the only one smart enough to see the problem and ruthless enough to solve it
- Set his sons against each other deliberately — promised the throne to whoever brings the seventh son
- A divided court is easier to control than a united one
- **Shakespearean parallels:** Lear (dividing power, rejecting his honest child), Macbeth (prophecy driving monstrous action), The Winter's Tale (paranoia destroying family)
- The townsfolk LIKE the king. They have good reason to want you captured. Some believe your death ensures their safety (an uncle told them). Some want nothing to do with you. Few are your allies

### The Father (Seventh Son, First Generation)
- The seventh son of the king. Grew up watching his father's obsession destroy his brothers
- Rejected knighthood entirely. Became a farmer. Fled, changed his name, married for love
- Had children because he wanted a family. Didn't count. Didn't plan. The seventh came anyway
- **His mistake:** denial. You can't outrun the things you refuse to face
- Told his six older sons to scatter and change their identities before disappearing himself
- **Disappeared** before the game begins — went to the dangerous outer lands seeking a third path: not seizing the power (grandfather's way) or denying it (his way), but UNDERSTANDING it
- He's looking for answers about what the magic actually is and what it costs
- Finding him is the mid-to-late game goal. Like Indiana Jones but less tropey — he's a farmer looking for wisdom, not an adventurer looking for treasure
- **Shakespearean parallel:** Prospero from The Tempest — exiled, living simply, grappling with power. Except he chose renunciation and it didn't work. The storm still came

### The Mother
- Knew about your eyes. Took care of them, hid them. Told you nothing about the prophecy
- When soldiers find your home, she tells you to hide. She is taken by Uncle Aldric
- She CANNOT reveal she's the seventh son's wife — that would confirm the seventh son exists and is nearby
- She endures captivity in silence to protect you. That's heroism without a sword
- Rescuing her is a mid-game goal (not the first dungeon — you're too young/weak at first)
- Once rescued, she becomes your anchor/home base and tells you about your brothers

### The Player Character
- 10-12 years old at game start
- **Mute** — born without speech. Silent protagonist. Player projects their own thoughts
- **Glowing magical eyes** — unmistakable, the thing everyone is hunting for
- Three layers of isolation: can't speak, pretends can't see, hides who he is
- NPCs talk TO you; you respond with gestures, nods, action choices
- Brothers and mother speak for you sometimes
- Dialogue choices are actions, not words

### The Blind Disguise
- Your glowing eyes mark you. To move undetected, you wear a **blindfold** and pretend to be blind
- With blindfold: appearance is straight brown hair, simple traveling clothes. Unassuming
- Without blindfold: hair grows instantaneously — golden or lavender, flowing. Eyes glow. Unmistakable
- You learn to fight blind using your seventh-son perception — "sight beyond sight"
- The disguise is your survival. Removing it has permanent consequences for that area

### Story Structure

**Opening:** Peaceful farm life. You have strange, beautiful eyes. Your mother tends to them lovingly. You don't know why. Soldiers arrive. Your mother is taken. You hide in a root cellar while they search. You are alone.

**First Act — Survival:** You're a child alone in the countryside. No dungeon. No quest. Just survival. You discover the blindfold trick out of necessity. You learn to fight with your fists. You may find a brother nearby. You set out into a world that doesn't want you.

**Rising Action:** Travel the kingdom. Grow stronger. Find brothers (optional). Learn about the prophecy piece by piece. Return to rescue your mother from Aldric (mid-game, once strong enough). She tells you about your other brothers and gives you the first father fragment. Defeat uncles in non-linear order. Each uncle reveals a piece of the larger truth.

**Moment of Despair:** You are captured. They take BOTH your eyes. Total darkness. Everything that defined you — gone.

**The Turn:** But you learned to move blind from years of wearing the blindfold. And you discover the power was never in your eyes — it was in your inner spirit. The eyes were just the expression, like a musician losing their instrument but still hearing the music. You learn to see with your spirit: truth, intention, fear, love, lies. MORE than the eyes ever showed.

**The Jester Scene:** After losing your eyes, you end up in the capital. A mute blind boy put to work as court jester — what's less threatening? Brought before the grandfather-king. Physical comedy. The court laughs AT you. A mute blind fool performing for the man who ordered your eyes taken. Dramatic irony: the player knows, the king doesn't. Then the reveal. The silence isn't comedy anymore — it's judgment. He can't speak but doesn't need to. *(Hamlet: "The play's the thing wherein I'll catch the conscience of the king.")*

**Resolution:** TBD — eyes healed through what means? What does the final confrontation with the grandfather look like? How does the kingdom change?

### The Three Goals (Player Motivation)
1. **Survive** → **Rescue your mother** (first act → mid-game)
2. **Find your father** (the mystery driving the mid-to-late game)
3. **End the grandfather's reign** (emerges as you understand the full picture)

## The Six Uncles

All are the king's sons, your father's brothers. Knights who serve the crown. Each hunts you for different reasons. The grandfather promised the throne to whoever brings the seventh son. They compete against each other as much as they hunt you.

Not all are villains. All are trapped by the grandfather's system.

**Each uncle's quest line can be resolved in multiple ways** — kill, spare, persuade, expose. Consequences ripple through the world. Defeating an uncle changes how that region's people treat you (some become friendlier, some become HOSTILE — they loved their lord).

### Uncle Aldric — The Seneschal
_"Aldric" = old ruler_
- **Domain:** The lowlands near your childhood home
- **Motivation:** Entitlement. He's the firstborn, believes everything is rightfully his. Takes credit for everything. Treats servants like furniture. Genuinely thinks he's a gift to the world
- **Has your mother** — doesn't know she's his brother's wife. She can't tell him
- **Challenge type:** Stealth and infiltration. His domain is well-organized, well-guarded. You must return here mid-game once strong enough. A dungeon you CANNOT beat at the start
- **Personality:** Hateable. The kind of person who talks about themselves in third person. Players should relish taking him down
- **What you learn:** Your mother tells you about your brothers. First father fragment
- **Reward:** Your mother freed (becomes home base). First major story revelation

### Uncle Maren — The Admiral
_"Maren" = sea_
- **Domain:** Dragon's Isle. Coastal/volcanic naval fortress
- **Motivation:** Wants the throne. Period. The most straightforwardly ambitious uncle. Has the biggest army and navy
- **Challenge type:** Combat gauntlet. Militaristic, aggressive, naval warfare themes
- **Personality:** Surprisingly honest about what he wants. Respects strength. Not cruel, just ruthless
- **What you learn:** The grandfather wasn't always like this. Maren remembers him as a great king. Shows you the human side of the antagonist
- **Reward:** **Dragon's Eye Amulet** — passive true-sight in a small radius WITHOUT removing blindfold. Detect hidden things while staying incognito. (Powerful — mid-game reward, not early)

### Uncle Voss — The Warden
_"Voss" = determined/fox_
- **Domain:** The Iron Citadel. Industrial fortress, mining, metalwork
- **Motivation:** Fear. Terrified of magic, terrified of the grandfather, terrified of his brothers. A coward who does terrible things because he's scared. Built anti-magic armor specifically because he's afraid of YOU
- **Challenge type:** Pure mundane skill. Magic doesn't work in his domain (anti-magic fields). Fists and steel only. The difficulty spike
- **Personality:** Pathetic in a pitiable way. A man who could have been decent if he weren't so afraid
- **What you learn:** One of your brothers is imprisoned here. Voss captured him years ago but doesn't know his identity — just a suspicious wanderer
- **Reward:** **Brother rescued** + **Ironforge access** (best weapon repair/crafting in the game). Knowledge that anti-magic technology exists (matters for endgame)

### Uncle Sable — The Naturalist
_"Sable" = dark/black, also the animal_
- **Domain:** The Drowned March. Swamplands, fog, decay
- **Motivation:** He secretly agreed with your father. Thought the prophecy was madness. But too cowardly to leave, so he retreated to the most remote domain and practices crude, dark nature magic (not true seventh-son magic — bastardized, cobbled from folklore)
- **Challenge type:** Exploration and moral choice. His domain isn't hostile by design — it's hostile because his bad magic warped the swamp and it's gotten out of control. He needs HELP, not defeat
- **Personality:** The sympathetic uncle. Tragic. The could-have-been. If he'd had your father's courage, he would have left too
- **What you learn:** Why your father really left. Sable was the last brother your father talked to. Knows the direction he went
- **Reward:** **Father's Trail** (unlocks path to outer lands) + **Nature's Tongue** (permanent animal communication — animals become informants and allies across the whole map)

### Uncle Corvin — The Inquisitor
_"Corvin" = raven (wisdom, omens, death)_
- **Domain:** The Hallowed Reach. Theocratic territory, churches, monasteries, devout populace
- **Motivation:** Religious conviction. Believes the prophecy is divine mandate and the seventh son MUST be brought to the king — not out of cruelty but zealous certainty that this is how the world is supposed to work. Intelligent, articulate, sincere. Completely wrong
- **Challenge type:** Social infiltration. His people report strangers willingly. Guards patrol out of righteousness, not orders. Children report you. Priests bless the soldiers. Everyone is a true believer. The stealth here isn't physical — it's ideological
- **Personality:** The most dangerous uncle because you can't just hate him. Calm, reasoned. Would have a thoughtful conversation about why you should surrender — and his arguments would be GOOD. The uncle kids will argue about
- **What you learn:** The full theoretical basis of the prophecy. What the scholars actually predicted. What the magic is SUPPOSED to be
- **Reward:** **The Confessor's Ear** — understand the TRUE meaning behind what anyone says. Lying NPCs reveal real dialogue. Afraid people show real feelings. Cheating merchants get exposed. Recontextualizes the whole game retroactively — revisit old NPCs and discover the world was more complicated than you thought

### Uncle Brandt — The Champion
_"Brandt" = sword/fire_
- **Domain:** No fixed domain. He's OUT THERE, actively hunting you
- **Motivation:** Direct orders from the grandfather. Doesn't think about it. The perfect soldier — loyal, skilled, empty inside. He gave up his own identity to serve. He is what your father WOULD have been if he'd stayed
- **Challenge type:** Recurring threat + final duel. He's mobile. You hear rumors of him: "The Champion passed through yesterday." You see the aftermath — burnt farms, interrogated villagers. Encounter escalation:
  1. You see him from a distance. Hide. Realize how outmatched you are
  2. He finds you. You barely escape (maybe a brother helps)
  3. Forced confrontation on **The Long Bridge** — narrow, nowhere to hide, single combat. The set piece from teenage notes
- **Personality:** Not evil. Not anything. A mirror showing what total obedience looks like
- **What you learn:** The grandfather's specific plan — the full endgame. Brandt knows everything as the king's right hand. He tells you after you beat him because he respects strength. A soldier following the code
- **Reward:** TBD — needs to be something that opens the endgame path. Brandt's personal weapon? Access to the capital? Information?

### Suggested Path (Non-Linear After First Act)
1. **First Act** — Survival in countryside (no dungeon)
2. **Aldric** — Return mid-game to rescue mother (stealth dungeon)
3. **Maren / Sable / Corvin / Voss** — any order, guided by difficulty and knowledge
4. **Brandt** — natural final uncle (recurring threat culminating in duel)
5. **Endgame** — The capital, the grandfather, the jester scene

## The Six Brothers

Your father told each to scatter and change identities. They left as children/teens. Most don't know where the others are. Finding them is **optional** but provides father fragments, items, quests, and allies.

Not all are friendly. Some are bitter. Some are lost.

### Brother Archetypes (names TBD)

**The Mercenary** — Angry. Dad ruined his life by making him hide. Doesn't care about prophecy or family. Wants money and freedom. Hostile until he learns who you are, and even then reluctant. Helps but charges you for it.

**The Captured Brother** — Raised by one of the uncles (Voss?) without knowing his true identity. Essentially a cousin now, loyal to the uncle. You have to REVEAL his identity to him. He may not believe you.

**The Traitor** — Your ally at first. Seems helpful. Gives you a father fragment. Then sells your location to an uncle. His motivation: he thinks if he delivers you, the grandfather will accept HIM as a legitimate heir. He wants the family he was denied. When you confront him, it's not a boss fight — it's a conversation. You can forgive him or not.

**The Protector** — The eldest. Took the father's charge seriously. Has been watching over you from a distance without you knowing. Appears at critical moments. The most reliable ally.

**The Scholar** — Went looking for answers like your father, but in libraries and archives instead of the wild. Knows the academic history of the prophecy. Provides lore and context.

**The Lost One** — Disappeared. Nobody knows where he is. Finding him is a late-game optional quest. He may be in the outer lands near your father, or somewhere unexpected.

Each brother gives a **father fragment** — a memory, a letter, a map piece, a keepsake. Collect enough and the path to the father becomes clear. (Gon and Ging Freecss energy, as you noted.)

## The Two Visions

Instead of a parallel "dark world" map, every area has two visual interpretations:

### Blindfold ON (Monk Mode / Normal Vision)
- The world as everyone else sees it
- Muted but functional — navigate, fight (fists), interact with NPCs
- Appearance: straight brown hair, blindfold, simple clothes
- Safe in towns, invisible to hunters
- CANNOT see: hidden doors, magical creatures, corruption, true forms
- CANNOT use: magic abilities

### Blindfold OFF (True Sight / Revealed)
- The hidden magical layer becomes visible
- Beautiful castle is actually rotting. Kind merchant is a spy. Peaceful lake hides something
- Appearance transforms: golden/lavender flowing hair (grows instantly), glowing eyes
- NPCs react with fear, guards alert, bounty increases, hunters sent
- Permanent consequences for revealing in populated areas: increased security, patrols, hunters on your tail
- Required for some puzzles → must complete quests to clear people from area first

### After Losing Eyes (Spirit Sight)
- Sees MORE than either previous mode
- Truth, intentions, emotions visible. Corruption as feeling, not visual overlay
- Transcends the previous limitation — you needed the eyes to see magic, but you need your spirit to see truth
- Visual representation TBD (abstract? ethereal? beautiful in a different way?)

### Consequence System
- Removing blindfold in hostile area = **permanent escalation** for that area
- Towns become more hostile, increased security, surrounding areas get patrols
- Hunters dispatched specifically for that region
- Creates stealth/planning gameplay: "I need true sight HERE, so first I clear THAT area"
- Far-reaching effects to all actions — the world remembers what you've done

## World

### Map Philosophy
- Large interconnected overworld, 3/4 top-down (Link to the Past style)
- Fantastical but **lived-in**. People farm, trade, argue, live their lives
- 6 uncle domains + neutral/wild zones + the capital
- Quest/story/puzzle driven progression — NOT heavily movement-gated
- No fast travel. You learn the world by walking it
- Secret areas, optional dungeons, brother hideouts
- The world is BIGGER than the kingdom — hints at lands beyond

### Regions

**Uncle Domains:**
- The Lowlands (Aldric) — pastoral, deceptively peaceful, well-guarded
- Dragon's Isle (Maren) — coastal/volcanic, naval fortress, fire and sea
- The Iron Citadel (Voss) — industrial, mining, anti-magic forges, oppressive
- The Drowned March (Sable) — swamplands, fog, warped nature, tragic beauty
- The Hallowed Reach (Corvin) — theocratic, churches, monasteries, devout populace

**Neutral/Wild Zones:**
- **The Crystal Forest** — ancient, pre-kingdom. Trees of living crystal. No uncle controls it. Sacred/dangerous. Crystal weapons found here. Maybe where the prophecy's scholars once studied
- **The Floating Isle** — ancient landmass chained to the earth for millennia. Sky library? Pilgrimage site? Where the theoretical prophecy was first written. Not a combat zone — a place of knowledge and wonder
- **The Bone Field** — desolate land of bones and skulls. A place where something terrible happened long ago. Walk across it. Feel the weight
- **The Long Bridge** — narrow, for single combat. Where you face Brandt
- **The Countryside** — your home, now destroyed. The starting area. Return here and it hits different each time
- **The Capital** — the grandfather's seat. The final destination
- **The Outer Lands** — dangerous territory beyond the kingdom's borders. Where your father went. Mythical creatures, ancient ruins, unknown dangers

**Worldbuilding:**
- **The Eastern Warband** — a group from a distant land, camped at the far edge of the map. Nobody expected them. They don't explain themselves. Friendly, trade with you, unique weapons and fighting styles. Maybe they're looking for something too — their own prophecy, their own quest. They hint that the world is vast and your kingdom's problems are small. A humbling encounter. No lore explanation needed — mystery is more interesting

## Gameplay

### Core Loop
1. Explore overworld → discover new area, NPC, quest
2. Navigate challenges (combat, stealth, social, environmental)
3. Confront uncle or uncover story beat
4. Gain perception/connection ability → see more of the world
5. Piece together the larger mystery

### Reward Philosophy
Most rewards are about **seeing more of the world** rather than traversing it differently. The map is physically accessible early — you just can't SEE everything. The hookshot equivalent is enlightenment. Thematically perfect.

| Source | Reward | What It Opens |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Aldric | Mother freed + first father fragment | Home base, brother locations, story |
| Maren | Dragon's Eye (passive mini true-sight) | Detect hidden things while incognito |
| Voss | Ironforge + freed brother | Best crafting, anti-magic knowledge |
| Sable | Nature's Tongue + father's trail | Animal allies everywhere, endgame path |
| Corvin | Confessor's Ear (understand true meaning) | Recontextualizes NPC dialogue across whole game |
| Brandt | TBD | Endgame access |
| Brothers | Father fragments (cumulative) | Path to father in outer lands |

### Combat — Three Enemy Types
1. **Natural** — flesh and blood. Melee/projectiles work. Die in a few hits after armor break
2. **Armored** — armor must be broken first. Projectiles mostly bounce (need rare armor-piercing arrows). Magic bypasses armor
3. **Magical** — can only be killed through magical means. Forces blindfold removal = the tension
- **Voss has magic-absorbing armor** — can't use magic OR mundane approaches easily. Unique strategies required

### Combat Styles

**Fist Moveset (Monk / Always Available):**
- Deep, counter-based, defensive martial arts
- Upgradeable through the game
- Your fallback when weapons break. Your primary when disguised

**Weapons:**
- Swords, axes, hammers, bows, crystal weapons
- Weapon durability is central (see below)
- Always cycling weapons, managing resources

**Magic (Blindfold Off Only → Spirit Mode Later):**
- Sight magic: reveals hidden things, sees through deception
- Nature magic: healing, purification, plant growth
- Animal communication: talk to animals, gain allies and information
- Peaceful and perceptive — NOT destructive (not Golden Sun-style TK/barriers)
- Using magic = eyes glow brighter = more visible = more danger

### Weapon Durability

| Material | Damage | Durability | Repair | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|--------|-------|
| Wood/Bone | Low | Low | Easy | Craftable anywhere |
| Iron/Steel | Medium | High | Medium | Needs forge |
| Crystal | High | Very Low | None | Shatters. Found in Crystal Forest |
| Enchanted | Varies | Medium | Special | Needs magic to repair |
| Fists | Low | ∞ | N/A | Always available, upgradeable |

### Stat System
- **STR, CON, INT, LCK, DEF, ATK**
- Gated by: enemies defeated, locations discovered, quests completed (NOT item use)
- Armor/weapons modify stats
- LCK affects drops
- Simple for kids, deep for adults

### Drop System
- Regular drop chance mechanics
- Hidden kill counter per enemy type: kill X without taking damage → forced drop
- Rewards skilled play invisibly

### Exploration & Stealth
- Quest/story/puzzle driven progression
- Incognito system: blindfold on = safe, can shop/talk/gather info
- Reveal system: blindfold off in populated area = permanent escalating consequences
- Some puzzles REQUIRE true sight → quest to clear NPCs from area first
- Brothers provide intel, safe houses, equipment, story
- No fast travel — learn the world by walking it

## Thematic Framework

### Shakespeare Parallels
- **King Lear:** Grandfather dividing power, rejecting his honest child, the Fool seeing truth the king cannot
- **The Tempest:** Father as Prospero — exiled, renouncing power, but the storm comes anyway
- **Hamlet:** Knowing something is rotten and being unable to speak it (literally mute). "The play's the thing" = jester scene
- **Macbeth:** Prophecy driving a man to monstrous action. The grandfather's philosophy
- **As You Like It:** Exiled nobility in disguise. Your brothers scattered like the Forest of Arden exiles
- **The Winter's Tale:** A king's paranoia destroying his family. The lost child who returns to heal the wound

### Core Themes
- **Adults make mistakes and must learn from them** (father's denial, grandfather's obsession)
- **Identity vs. disguise** (who are you when you can't be yourself?)
- **Family as both strength and threat** (internecine — mutually destructive family conflict)
- **Power taken by force vs. power freely given** (dark magic vs. natural resonance)
- **Seeing truth vs. seeing surface** (blindfold → true sight → spirit sight)
- **You are more than what defines you** (the power was never in the eyes)
- **Silence is not weakness** (muteness as strength, the jester's judgment)
