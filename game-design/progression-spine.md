# Progression Spine — Genesis

## How This Works

Every unlock falls into one of three gate types:
- **🏃 MOVEMENT** — New traversal ability. Opens physical paths.
- **⚔️ COMBAT** — New weapon, damage type, or combat ability. Opens enemy gates (things you couldn't kill before).
- **🧠 KNOWLEDGE** — EVE scan data, native translations, lore fragments. Opens understanding gates (things you couldn't interact with, translate, or decode).

The Tree of Knowledge is literal: knowledge gates are the unique hook. Some doors don't need a key — they need you to *know something*. EVE's scan database IS your progression tracker.

---

## The Knowledge Tree (Core Hook)

Knowledge isn't XP. It's a branching tree of understanding with four roots:

### 🌿 Ecology (Creature Knowledge)
- Scanning creatures reveals behavior, weakness, biology
- Higher scan levels: internal anatomy → lifecycle → ecosystem role
- **Gate examples:** "This creature only surfaces during seismic activity" → you need to understand Burrower biology to trigger the encounter that opens the Petrified Forest shortcut

### 🏛️ Archaeology (Ruins Knowledge)  
- Scanning ruins/artifacts reveals history, tech, language
- The pre-native civilization left infrastructure that IS the terrain
- **Gate examples:** "This door mechanism uses a pressure system based on water weight" → you need to have scanned enough ruins to decode the engineering

### 🗣️ Linguistics (Native Knowledge)
- EVE learns the native language through exposure
- Partial translations → full translations → cultural understanding
- **Gate examples:** A native tells you about a passage, but you can only understand them if EVE's translation is far enough along. The passage was always there — you just didn't know.

### ⚡ Technology (Hybrid Knowledge)
- Understanding how Federation tech + planetary materials interact
- Unlocks hybrid crafting recipes
- **Gate examples:** You find a Federation battery. Useless. But if you've scanned enough planetary crystals AND understood enough Federation tech logs, EVE synthesizes a hybrid power cell that opens the Void Rift entrance.

### How Knowledge Gates Feel Different
- A movement gate: "I can see the ledge but can't reach it" → get double jump → reach it
- A combat gate: "This enemy is immune to my weapon" → get fire damage → kill it  
- A knowledge gate: "I walked past this ten times. I just realized what it means." → the world hasn't changed, YOU have

This is the Elden Ring meteor moment you described. The world reshapes because your understanding reshapes.

## Act 1: The Fall (Eden Reach → Briarwood)

### Beat 1 — Prologue (Playable)
- **Location:** Adam's ship, then space combat
- **Gameplay:** Ship controls tutorial. Fight the Mecha Dragon. YOU LOSE.
- **Key moment:** Dragon says something cryptic. EVE crash-lands you.
- **Unlock:** Nothing. You lose everything.

### Beat 2 — Awakening
- **Location:** Eden Reach — crash site
- **Gameplay:** Wake up. Find a stick. Kill your first Crawlers.
- **NPC:** EVE (damaged, limited functionality)
- **Unlock:** 🏃 Basic movement, ⚔️ Stick (melee)
- **Knowledge:** EVE scan tutorial — scan a tree, learn it has structural leaves

### Beat 3 — First Shelter
- **Location:** Eden Reach — near crash site
- **Gameplay:** Gather leaves (from scanned trees), build shelter. First build phase.
- **NPC:** EVE guides you through crafting
- **Unlock:** 🏛️ Shelter (save point + storage), ⚔️ Axe (crafted)
- **Knowledge:** 🌿 First creature scans (Crawlers). 🏛️ Ship debris scans reveal Federation data logs.

### Beat 4 — First Expansion
- **Location:** Eden Reach — outer zones
- **Gameplay:** Clear Thornback walls blocking the path east. Combat tutorial deepens.
- **Enemy:** Thornbacks (stationary blockers), Crawler packs
- **Unlock:** ⚔️ Sling (ranged), found in ship debris
- **Knowledge:** 🌿 Thornback scan reveals they grow from specific soil. You can prevent regrowth by altering the ground (building on it).

### Beat 5 — The Briarwood Gate
- **Location:** Eden Reach → Briarwood border
- **Boss:** Nephilim Spawn (first mini-boss). Patrols the border.
- **Gameplay:** Souls-like learnable patterns. Telegraphed but punishing.
- **Unlock:** 🏃 Wall climb (learned from observing how the Nephilim scaled the cliff — you adapt)
- **NPC:** First native sighting in the distance. They flee.
- **Knowledge:** 🧠 Scanning the Nephilim reveals it's a hybrid — partially corrupted by Dragon influence. First hint that corruption is spreading.

### Beat 6 — The Briarwood
- **Location:** Briarwood (vertical forest biome)
- **Gameplay:** Wall climbing exploration. Wingbeaters introduced (aerial threats).
- **Enemy:** Wingbeaters (dive-bombers), Crawlers in canopy
- **Unlock:** ⚔️ Bow (crafted from Briarwood materials)
- **Knowledge:** 🌿 Wingbeater scan reveals migration patterns. They avoid certain areas. Why?
- **Discovery:** Native ruins in the canopy — first archaeological scans.

### Beat 7 — First Contact
- **Location:** Briarwood — native village
- **NPC:** Natives. They speak, but EVE can barely translate. Fragments only.
- **Gameplay:** Diplomacy attempt. Gestures. Misunderstandings. They're cautious but not hostile.
- **Unlock:** 🗣️ Linguistics root opens in Knowledge Tree. First native word fragments.
- **Knowledge:** The natives have a word for "the fire in the sky" — the Dragon. They've been living with this threat. You're not the first to fight it.

## Act 2: Genesis (Petrified Forest → Crystal Reach)

### Beat 8 — Settlement Grows
- **Location:** Eden Reach settlement
- **Gameplay:** Major building phase. Assign NPCs to roles. Unlock the crucible (crafting station).
- **NPC:** 3-5 settlers have joined. Each has a role: builder, farmer, scout, etc.
- **Unlock:** 🏛️ Crucible (advanced crafting), NPC roles system
- **Knowledge:** 🌿 Grazer scan reveals they're docile and their dung fertilizes crops. Settlement benefit — don't kill them.

### Beat 9 — Petrified Forest
- **Location:** Petrified Forest
- **Gate:** 🧠 Ecology — you need to understand Burrowers (from scan data) to navigate. They create the tunnels that are the only path through.
- **Enemy:** Burrowers (ambush from below), breakable floors
- **Gameplay:** Claustrophobic, vertical-down. EVE's scan reveals Burrower tunnels before you fall in.
- **Unlock:** 🏃 Down-dash / ground pound (learned from how Burrowers move — a recurring theme: abilities come from understanding)
- **Knowledge:** 🏛️ Deep scans of the petrified trees reveal they're not trees. They're the bones of something enormous. Pre-native civilization? The creature whose body the natives built in?

### Beat 10 — Cain and Abel
- **Location:** Eden Reach settlement
- **NPC:** Two settlers. One productive, beloved (Abel analog). One jealous, bitter (Cain analog).
- **Gameplay:** Story beat plays out in settlement. Cain kills Abel. You find the body.
- **Choice:** How do you handle Cain? Exile? Execute? Imprison? Forgive?
- **Consequence:** Your choice affects settler morale and a late-game callback.
- **Knowledge:** 🗣️ If your linguistics is advanced enough, a native elder comments on the killing. Their culture has a myth about "the first murder." Another parallel. Why?

### Beat 11 — The World Opens
- **Location:** Multiple paths available
- **Gameplay:** Non-linear. You can go to Coral Heights OR push deeper into the Petrified Forest OR explore Briarwood secrets.
- **Design:** No explicit direction. Exploration IS the game now. Let the player wander.
- **Knowledge gates start mattering:** Some paths require scan data you may or may not have. Revisiting old areas with new knowledge reveals new things.

### Beat 12 — Coral Heights
- **Location:** Coral Heights (aerial biome)
- **Gate:** 🏃 Wall climb + bow (for Wingbeater nests blocking the path)
- **Enemy:** Wingbeaters (home turf), Floodwalker (first appearance — absorbing creatures below)
- **Gameplay:** Momentum-based platforming. Bouncy organic platforms. Rope climbing.
- **Unlock:** 🏃 Double jump / air dash (Dagger found here enables it)
- **NPC:** A native hermit lives in the heights. Speaks more clearly (EVE's translation improving). Tells you about the Crystal Reach — "the place where the world remembers."

### Beat 13 — The Crystal Reach
- **Location:** Crystal Reach (above-ground crystal biome)
- **Gate:** 🧠 Archaeology — the entrance is a light-puzzle using crystal refraction. You need to have scanned enough ruins to understand pre-native optical engineering.
- **Visual:** Trees made of crystal. Rivers of liquid crystal. Light bends through everything, creating rainbow caustics. Alien and breathtaking.
- **Enemy:** New creature — Prisms? Reflective enemies that redirect your ranged attacks.
- **Unlock:** ⚔️ Crystal-tipped weapons (new damage type), 🧠 Massive archaeology cache
- **Knowledge:** The crystals store data. Like a geological hard drive. EVE can read them. They contain fragments of the pre-native civilization's records. THEIR creation myth. A third account.
- **Discovery:** The pre-natives didn't die. They *became* the terrain. Their bodies crystallized. The Crystal Reach is a cemetery and a library simultaneously.

### Beat 14 — The Altar
- **Location:** Crystal Reach — deep interior
- **NPC:** Natives have a sacred site here. They ask Adam to perform a ritual.
- **Gameplay:** During the ritual, Federation technology is INSTANTLY drained. Not gradual corrosion — active suppression. The planet isn't passively hostile to tech. It's *actively* rejecting it. And the Crystal Reach is where that force is strongest.
- **Knowledge:** 🧠 This reframes EVERYTHING. The constraint isn't natural. It's intentional. Something or someone WANTS technology to fail here. Why?

## Act 3: Revelation (Ashlands → Depths)

### Beat 15 — Dragon Incursion
- **Location:** Eden Reach settlement
- **Gameplay:** The Dragon attacks your settlement. Not a full boss fight — a raid. Enemies swarm. Buildings can be damaged. NPCs can be hurt.
- **Consequence:** Permanent changes to settlement based on what survives. Rebuilding is part of the story.
- **Knowledge:** Dragon worshippers among your settlers may have enabled the attack. Who? Investigation gameplay.

### Beat 16 — Ashlands
- **Location:** Ashlands (corruption zone)
- **Gate:** ⚔️ Hybrid armor (crafted from crystal + Federation tech + native materials). Without it, the corruption damages you over time.
- **Enemy:** Corrupted versions of familiar creatures. Crawlers with mechanical augmentation. Thornbacks that shoot spines.
- **Gameplay:** Sprint-based. Damage tiles. Time pressure. Safe zones are scarce.
- **Unlock:** 🧠 Technology root deepens. Federation tech works BETTER near the corruption (double-edged). Guns don't degrade here. But enemies are vastly stronger.
- **Knowledge:** The corruption isn't destruction — it's transformation. The Dragon isn't killing the planet. It's *converting* it. Into what?

### Beat 17 — EVE's Confession
- **Location:** Ashlands — Federation wreckage site (another ship, much older)
- **NPC:** EVE finds data from a previous Federation expedition. They came before. They failed. EVE has fragments of their AI's logs.
- **Revelation:** EVE didn't just crash-land randomly. She aimed for this planet. She knew. Not everything — but she knew this planet was special.
- **Knowledge:** 🧠 This is the Temptation. EVE had hidden knowledge. She made a choice for you. Do you forgive her?

### Beat 18 — The Depths
- **Location:** The Depths (underground ruins)
- **Gate:** 🧠 Archaeology + Linguistics — you need full ruin decoding AND native guide knowledge. A native elder leads you to the entrance but won't go inside.
- **Gameplay:** Horror zone. Low visibility. Limited ammo. Slow. Dread.
- **Enemy:** Floodwalker (evolved, massive). The Watcher appears here — watching, never attacking.
- **Unlock:** 🧠 Complete pre-native history. Their technology. Their fall. Their transformation.
- **Knowledge:** The pre-natives built the constraint. They CHOSE to suppress technology after their own civilization collapsed from it. The planet's tech-resistance is their last gift — a quarantine against repeating their mistake. The Dragon is what happens when something resists the quarantine.

### Beat 19 — The Watcher Speaks
- **Location:** The Depths — deepest point
- **NPC:** The Watcher. It's not an enemy. It's a pre-native. Still alive, crystallized, watching.
- **Gameplay:** No combat. A conversation (if your knowledge is deep enough to understand it).
- **Knowledge:** The Watcher tells you what the Dragon actually is. Not a monster. A TOOL. Built by the pre-natives to protect the planet. It malfunctioned. It's been "protecting" the planet by converting everything into itself — a corrupted version of its original purpose.
- **Gate:** This knowledge changes how the Dragon encounter works. Without it, you fight. With it, you have options.

## Act 4: Transformation (Void Rift → Dragon's Axis)

### Beat 20 — Void Rift
- **Location:** Void Rift (ground zero)
- **Gate:** 🧠 ALL FOUR knowledge roots must be sufficiently advanced. Ecology + Archaeology + Linguistics + Technology. The entrance is a puzzle that requires understanding from every domain.
- **Gameplay:** Reality glitches. Normal rules break. Gravity shifts. Time loops. The planet's constraint is failing here.
- **Enemy:** Everything. Amped to 11.
- **Unlock:** Depends on your knowledge state (see endings).

### Beat 21 — The Dragon (Final)
- **Location:** Dragon's Axis
- **Gameplay:** Multi-phase boss fight. How it plays depends on what you know:
  - **Low knowledge:** Pure combat. Kill the Dragon. Hard. Ending A.
  - **High knowledge:** You can communicate with the Dragon's original programming. Mid-fight, a knowledge gate opens. You can choose to repair it instead of destroy it. Ending B.
  - **Max knowledge:** You understand everything. The pre-natives, the Dragon, the constraint, EVE's choice, the planet's purpose. A third option appears. Ending C.

## Endings

### Ending A — The Conqueror
- Kill the Dragon. Build your civilization. Stay on the planet.
- The constraint remains. Technology stays suppressed. You rule, but you don't understand.
- Thematically: You won the fight but missed the point.
- **Satisfying but incomplete.** The player knows they missed something.

### Ending B — The Successor (CANON)
- Repair the Dragon. It resumes its original purpose — protecting the planet properly.
- Adam becomes the new steward. The constraint adjusts — hybrid technology thrives.
- EVE integrates with the planetary systems. She becomes something more.
- Thematically: The Fall was the path to understanding. You had to lose everything to become whole.
- **The "true" ending.**

### Ending C — The Architect (Hidden)
- Requires max knowledge. You realize the planet is a SEED. One of many. Scattered across the galaxy.
- The pre-natives were gardeners. The Dragon was a caretaker. The constraint is an incubator.
- Adam and EVE can leave — not back to the Federation, but to the next seed planet.
- Thematically: The story of Genesis repeats everywhere. Creation is fractal.
- **Teases a larger universe. Sequel hook.**

| Neglect a cleared zone | Enemies reclaim it. Structures degrade. NPCs leave. | Maintenance cost to expansion. Can't just steamroll. |

## Unique Biome Notes

### The Crystal Reach (Ethan's Vision)
- **NOT a crystal cave/mine.** It's an above-ground crystal biome.
- Crystal trees with fractal branches. Crystal rivers that flow like liquid glass.
- Light refracts through everything — rainbow caustics on the ground.
- The crystals SING when struck. Different pitches. Musical puzzle potential.
- The "cheese instead of crystal mine" principle: this is memorable because it breaks the trope. Crystal biomes are usually underground and dark. This one is above ground, blindingly beautiful, and *alive.*
- Mechanically: light-based puzzles, reflective enemies, refraction as a tool.
- Thematically: the cemetery/library. Beauty built on death. The pre-natives didn't rot — they crystallized into something transcendent.

### Biome Identity (Ethan's "Unique Tropes" Note)
Every biome should subvert the expected trope:
- Forest biome → **Bioluminescent vertical jungle** (not green and flat)
- Ice biome → **Crystal Reach** (not cold, not underground — transcendent and terrifying)
- Fire biome → **Ashlands** (not lava — corrupted earth, Sprint-based survival, not combat-heavy)
- Water biome → **Coral Heights** (not underwater — organic coral growing into the sky, aerial)
- Cave biome → **The Depths** (horror, not exploration — you don't WANT to be here)
- Final zone → **Void Rift** (reality itself breaking, not just "harder enemies")
