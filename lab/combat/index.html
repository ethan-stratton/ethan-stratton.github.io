Wait Combat

← strattao · liquid · terrain · inspired by Helen's Mysterious Castle

Every action has three stats: Effectiveness (damage or healing), Defend (flat damage reduction while waiting), and Wait (ticks until the action fires). You defend with whatever you chose while you're waiting. Powerful attacks leave you exposed. Fast attacks keep you safe but deal less. Read the enemy's timer and choose wisely.

How it works

Based on Helen's Mysterious Castle (2014), a brilliant RPGMaker game that proved three numbers are enough for deep combat.

Damage = attacker's Effectiveness − defender's Defend. If Defend ≥ Effect, the attack is fully blocked (0 damage). Magic pierces defense entirely — Defend doesn't apply.

Wait counts down simultaneously for both sides. The first to hit 0 acts, then picks a new action. If both hit 0 on the same tick, you go first. A Metronome (Wait: 1) lets you peek at the enemy's choice before committing.

The tension: A heavy sword hits hard but its high Wait means you eat multiple fast attacks while charging. A dagger is safe but tickles armored enemies. A shield blocks everything but deals no damage. A spell ignores armor but leaves you at 0 Defend. Every pick is a tradeoff.

Enemies have AI patterns — some react to your choices. The Lizardman raises its guard when it sees you winding up an attack. The Dark Knight punishes slow spells with fast counters.
