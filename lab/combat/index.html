Matrimony Beyond Space and Time

← strattao · combat prototype · inspired by Helen's Mysterious Castle

A long time ago, everything was connected. Stone knew fire. Wind remembered water. People called it the Matrimony — not a metaphor, but the living union of all things. Then something severed it. Nobody alive remembers what.

Now the world is quiet. Ruins hum with frequencies only a few can hear. Weapons remember their owners. Creatures wander alone, not evil — just lonely. You woke up in this world with no memory, carrying a blade that feels like it knows you. A kid named Evan found you. He can't hear the frequencies. He stays human so you don't have to.

This is a combat prototype. The system below is the foundation — everything else grows from it.

Eff = damage. Def = damage reduction while waiting. Wait = ticks until your action fires. Both sides count down simultaneously. Powerful attacks leave you exposed. Fast attacks keep you safe.

Combat Mechanics

Damage = Eff − Def. If Def ≥ Eff, fully blocked. Pierce ignores defense. Stun adds wait ticks. Absorb heals for damage dealt. Seal locks the target's current action for one turn. Invigorate permanently buffs all your Eff (+5, stacks).

The tension: Shields get pierced. Slow spells get stunned. Heals get rushed. Turtling gets sealed. No single strategy wins — read the enemy, count ticks, adapt.

Transformations

Transformations change HOW you interact with the Eff / Def / Wait triangle — not just stat bonuses. Each one redefines your relationship to the combat system. These are not implemented yet — this is the design spec.

🧛 Vampire

Your Def stat doesn't work normally. Instead you have a Blood meter (starts at 0, max 30). Taking damage fills Blood (1:1 with damage taken). Spend Blood to supercharge your next action:

  • 10 Blood: Next attack gets 2× Eff
  • 20 Blood: Next attack gets 2× Eff AND heals you for damage dealt
  • 30 Blood: Next attack gets 3× Eff, heals, AND stuns

You WANT to get hit by small attacks. You FEAR big hits. Metronome becomes your best friend — Wait 1 tick to scout if the incoming hit is worth eating. Shield becomes a panic button, not a default. Drain Touch synergy: already heals, now also fills Blood. The sustain loop.

🐺 Werewolf

You lose access to all weapons. Replaced with 3 innate actions: Claw (Eff 12, Def 3, Wait 3), Bite (Eff 18, Def 0, Wait 5, absorb), Howl (Eff 0, Def 0, Wait 4, stun 4).

BUT: you act TWICE per cycle. After your action resolves, immediately pick again before the enemy's next action. Raw tempo. The decision space shrinks but the pace doubles.

Weakness: no shield, no heal, no range. Pure aggression on a timer.

💀 Lich

All your attacks become pierce. Your Heal is replaced with Soul Harvest (Eff 0, Wait 8 — kills any enemy below 15% HP instantly, otherwise fizzles).

BUT: your max HP ticks down by 1 every 3 ticks. You're on a clock. Every fight is a DPS race against your own decay. Invigorate becomes critical — buff fast, kill fast, or die to yourself. Long fights are death sentences. Shield still works (you need it), but you can't heal. Drain Touch is your only sustain.

🧟 Revenant

You can't be killed — when HP hits 0, you resurrect at 15 HP (once per fight).

BUT: after resurrection, all your Wait values are doubled. You're slow, desperate, burning. The strategic question: do you play safe to avoid triggering resurrection? Or do you kamikaze early with high-risk plays, knowing you have a safety net, then survive the slow phase?

Invigorate before death carries over. Setup → die → resurrect → execute with buffed weapons.

🔮 Chronomancer

You can see the enemy's chosen action before picking yours (it's revealed immediately).

BUT: your Wait values are all +3. You're always slower. Perfect information, imperfect tempo. You know they're charging Fire Breath — but can you get your Shield up in time? Or do you Stun Mace to delay them further? Metronome at Wait 4 instead of 1. Even scouting costs something.

⚡ Berserker

Each consecutive attack (non-shield, non-heal) adds +3 Eff to the NEXT attack (stacks). Using Shield or Heal resets the streak to 0.

The snowball: Longbow (+0) → Longsword (+3) → Broadsword (+6) → Thunder (+9) = 44 pierce damage.

The tension: you can't afford to defend or heal without losing your streak. Pure offense rewarded, but one bad read and you're eating damage with no defense and no way to heal without resetting your damage engine.

Transformation Interface Shifts

Each transformation doesn't just change your stats — it changes what you see. The interface itself shifts to reflect your new nature. Information is power in this system, so changing what information you have access to changes how you play fundamentally.

🧛 Vampire — Dark Sight

The screen darkens. Enemy sprites gain a faint red pulse showing their "heartbeat" — a visual tell that speeds up when they're low HP. You can feel when they're close to death without reading numbers. The blood meter glows brighter as it fills, lighting the screen. At 30 Blood, the whole arena has a crimson cast. You're not reading stats anymore — you're hunting.

🐺 Werewolf — Instinct

Eff/Def/Wait numbers are hidden. Actions show only names and a vague feel: "Heavy," "Fast," "Desperate." You're playing on instinct. The enemy's wait bar still ticks, but you don't see the number — just the bar shrinking. Fights are faster, wilder, less calculated. This is the opposite of Chronomancer.

💀 Lich — Death's Eyes

All living enemies appear as skeletons — sprites shift to bone. The world looks decayed, colors desaturated. Enemy HP is shown as an exact number (not a bar), but YOUR HP bar has no number — just a bar that might be lying to you (it drifts slightly, ±3 HP visual noise). You see death clearly. Your own mortality is uncertain.

🔮 Chronomancer — Foresight

You see the enemy's current AND next queued action. Two moves ahead. The second action is shown faintly, ghosted — it might change if the enemy's AI reacts to what you do. But it gives you a plan. The tradeoff: your +3 Wait penalty means you're always reacting from behind. You see the future but can't keep up with it.

🧟 Revenant — Afterimage

After resurrection, the screen flickers. Your sprite glitches — stuttering frames, brief static. The combat log occasionally shows corrupted text. You're not fully here anymore. The doubled Wait values aren't just a penalty — they feel like lag. Like the game itself is struggling to keep you alive.

⚡ Berserker — Tunnel Vision

As your combo streak grows, the edges of the screen darken (vignette tightens). At 3+ streak, only the enemy and your action choices are fully visible. The combat log fades. Your own HP bar shrinks. You're losing awareness of everything except the next hit. Breaking the streak with Shield or Heal snaps the view back to normal — the sudden clarity feels like waking up.

Action Combos

Using specific actions in sequence triggers combo effects — bonus damage, status effects, or defensive reactions. These are NOT listed in the UI. Players discover them through experimentation, accident, and word of mouth. The game acknowledges a combo with a brief flash and a unique log message.

Design principle: Combos reward planning but don't obsolete single actions. A combo should be ~30-50% stronger than using the actions separately, not 5×. And every combo has a risk window — the setup action usually leaves you exposed.

Weapon Combos

  • Stun → any attack: "Shattered Guard" — bonus +5 Eff (enemy is staggered, defense disrupted)
  • Seal → pierce attack: "Exploit" — bonus +8 Eff (sealed their defense, now pierce through the gap)
  • Longbow → Longbow → Longbow: "Volley" — third arrow hits for 2× Eff (sustained pressure rewarded)
  • Any weapon 3× in a row: "Mastery" — third use gets +4 Eff (you're in a rhythm)
  • Trusty Dagger → Longsword: "Riposte" — Longsword gains pierce (dagger finds the gap, sword follows through)

Defensive Combos

  • Shield → Broadsword: "Shield Bash" — Broadsword Eff += damage blocked last turn (hit them with what they gave you)
  • Heal → Shield: "Fortify" — Shield Def +6 this turn (catching your breath behind steel)
  • Shield → Shield: "Brace" — second Shield Def +10 (digging in, but you're doing zero damage)

Magic Combos

  • Invigorate → Thunder: "Overcharge" — Thunder gains +10 Eff AND stuns 3 (channeled lightning)
  • Invigorate → Invigorate: "Surge" — second buff gives +8 instead of +5 (momentum)
  • Drain Touch → Seal Rune: "Soul Bind" — seal duration lasts 2 cycles instead of 1 (you've tasted their essence)

Transformation Synergies

Each transformation unlocks unique combo paths that don't exist in base form. These are the deepest secrets — requiring both the right transformation AND the right action sequence.

  • Vampire + Drain Touch → any attack: "Blood Frenzy" — next attack Eff scales with current Blood meter (10 Blood = +10 Eff)
  • Werewolf + Claw → Bite: "Savage" — Bite Eff doubled if Claw hit this cycle (pounce then devour)
  • Lich + Thunder → Thunder: "Chain Lightning" — second Thunder costs 0 Def, hits for 1.5× (death accelerates)
  • Berserker + 5-streak → Broadsword: "Deathblow" — Eff tripled but streak resets AND you take 10 self-damage (all-in finisher)
  • Chronomancer + Seal (on predicted action): "Paradox" — if you seal the action Chronomancer revealed as "next," seal lasts 3 cycles (you broke their timeline)
  • Revenant + any action at 1 HP: "Defiance" — Eff doubled (fighting with nothing left to lose)
Weapon Identity & Enchantments

Weapons aren't disposable tools — they grow, remember, and develop personality. Two players' Longswords should feel different after 20 fights.

Weapon Experience

Each weapon tracks usage. Every N uses, it evolves — not just +Eff, but new capabilities. Evolution paths are partially random (enchantments) and partially earned (combo discoveries).

  • Longsword Lv.1→2: "Keen Edge" — gains a 20% chance to pierce on any hit
  • Longsword Lv.2→3: "Blade Mastery" — Wait reduced by 1 permanently
  • Longbow Lv.1→2: "Steady Aim" — Def +2 while waiting (you've learned to hold position)
  • Longbow Lv.2→3: "Double Nock" — 30% chance to fire twice (second hit at half Eff)
  • Wooden Shield Lv.2→3: "Ironbound" — becomes Iron Shield, Def +4, enables Shield Bash combo

Random Enchantments

At evolution milestones, weapons gain a random enchantment from a pool. These are permanent and unique to your copy. Enchantments are named with mystical language — the weapon is becoming something with a history.

  • ...of the Comet: +3 Eff on first use each fight (opening strike)
  • ...of Echoes: 15% chance to repeat the action at half stats (ghostly afterimage)
  • ...of Stillwater: +3 Def permanently (the weapon has a calming presence)
  • ...of the Pyre: hits have 20% chance to add burn (2 damage per tick for 3 ticks)
  • ...of Famine: absorb 2 HP on any hit regardless of weapon type
  • ...of Thorns: attacker takes 3 damage when they hit you while this weapon is active
  • ...of Whispers: reveals enemy's next action for 1 turn after this weapon fires
  • ...of the Void: pierce, but Def becomes 0 for this action

The mystical feeling: Finding "Longsword of the Pyre" should feel like finding a named weapon in Dark Souls, not rolling a stat. The name tells a story. Why does this sword burn? Who used it before you? The game doesn't answer. Your imagination does.

Awakening (Beet-style)

At the highest weapon mastery, a weapon can Awaken — a one-time permanent transformation that changes its identity. Like Beet's Saiga manifesting from spiritual energy, an Awakened weapon reflects the player's journey with it.

Awakening depends on what you did with the weapon — kill count, combo count, damage taken while it was active, enemies sealed with it. Different histories = different Awakenings.

  • Longsword (high kill count): Awakens into "Oathblade" — Eff scales with consecutive victories (run momentum)
  • Longsword (high damage taken): Awakens into "Scar" — Eff increases as your HP drops (wounded fury)
  • Shield (many blocks): Awakens into "Aegis" — auto-blocks the first hit each fight at half Def (it remembers its purpose)
  • Drain Touch (high absorb total): Awakens into "Leech King" — absorb always heals at least 5, even on 0-damage blocked hits
  • Thunder (used with Invigorate): Awakens into "Judgment" — pierce + stun 2 baseline. The nuke.

The Beet connection: Beet's weapons aren't things he found. They're extensions of his will. The Saiga don't work for anyone else. An Awakened weapon in Wait Combat shouldn't feel like loot — it should feel like something that grew out of the player's choices. "This is MY Oathblade. It exists because of how I played." That's the attachment.

The Clash

When both combatants' Wait timers hit 0 on the same tick, a Clash occurs. Both actions resolve simultaneously, but with bonus effects:

  • Both attacking: Both deal +50% Eff. A mutual kill is possible. High risk, high drama.
  • Attack vs Shield: Attacker breaks through — damage ignores half of Def. Shields aren't safe at zero.
  • Attack vs Heal: Heal resolves first, then damage hits. The heal saves you — barely.
  • Both shielding: Nothing happens. A wasted turn for both. The game punishes passivity.

The design: Clashes are uncommon — you'd have to deliberately time your Wait to match the enemy's countdown. But when they happen, they're memorable. A Clash kill should feel like a finishing blow in a fighting game. Screen flash. Bigger damage popup. The log says something different: "CLASH — Longsword strikes true!" not just "hits for 22."

Strategic depth: Players who master Wait counting can engineer Clashes intentionally. See the enemy at Wait 6? Pick an action with Wait 6. You're betting you can win the simultaneous exchange. That's the "finisher" energy — not random, but earned through reading the fight.

World: Matrimony Beyond Space and Time

The Premise

A long time ago — so long that no one alive remembers — everything in the world was joined. Matter and spirit. Living and dead. Space and time. Not a utopia — just whole. Then something severed. A Divorce on a cosmic scale. The bonds between all things weakened. What's left is a world of beautiful, strange ruins — not ruined buildings, but ruined connections. Things that were once joined now sit next to each other, unable to fully touch.

Nobody knows what caused the Divorce. Nobody witnessed it. It's like plate tectonics — it shaped everything, but nobody thinks about it while eating breakfast. The sky has things in it that shouldn't be there — rings, debris, something that might be a station. Trees grow through ancient reactors and their sap glows. A nautilus shell fossil in a canyon wall might be natural, might be engineered — enough time passed that the distinction stopped mattering. Technology and nature merged. The planet grew around its own ruins.

The tone: Not grimdark. Not hopeless. Think Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind meets Beet the Vandel Buster. The world is vast, strange, and melancholy in its bones — but the people in it are alive. They argue about whose turn it is to cook. They fall in love with terrible people. They name their weapons embarrassing things. They make poop jokes after eating glowing mushrooms. Life goes on. Life ALWAYS goes on. The adventure is fun. The mystery is thrilling. The depth is there for those who look. The surface is a hell of a ride for everyone else.

The Aesthetic: Deep Time

Nautilus shells. Ancient mythical weapons. Spaceships that nobody flies anymore. The world evokes layers of time collapsed into a single moment. A sword forged from a meteorite that was actually a satellite that was actually a seed from somewhere else. Reactor-trees that hum with old energy. Fossilized machines in canyon walls. The sky holds debris from an age nobody remembers. Space is present but unreachable — like the ocean in ancient mythology. Vast, real, full of unknowns, and not for you. Yet.

References: Nausicaa (Miyazaki), Angel's Egg (Oshii), Moebius illustrations, Breath of Fire: Dragon Quarter, the quiet awe of standing in someone else's ruins and knowing they were greater than you. But also: the energy of shonen adventure, the thrill of discovery, the joy of hitting something really hard with a cool sword.

The Bonding Rush

Recently — within living memory — people discovered that some individuals can Soul Bond with ancient relics. Weapons, machines, fragments of old tech. For millennia these were just weird artifacts sitting in ruins. Now suddenly people are resonating with them. Nobody knows why it's happening now.

This kicked off something like a gold rush:

  • Relic Hunters — adventurers diving into deep ruins looking for bondable artifacts. Some are noble. Some are idiots. Some are both. This is your player.
  • Bonded Factions — groups of bonded individuals organized around shared interests. Not nations — more like guilds, crews, gangs. They control territory around major ruin sites. Each has culture, goals, rivalries, and internal drama.
  • The Unbonded Majority — most people can't bond. They have opinions about people who can. Some are awed. Some resentful. Some are trying to sell you fake relics. Some are sharp kids without powers who stick around anyway.

The Villain: Forced Matrimony

Someone — a person, a group, an intelligence — is systematically acquiring the most powerful relics. Not always through violence. Through trade, persuasion, theft, charm. They're assembling something. Building toward a completed Matrimony — the reunion of everything that was severed.

Sounds noble, right? That's the twist. Their goal makes sense. The world IS broken. Connection IS weakened. Reunion WOULD fix it. But they want to do it for everyone, whether everyone wants it or not. Forced matrimony. Forced union. The obliteration of individuality in the name of wholeness. No more separation, no more loneliness — but also no more self. No more choice. No more sitting at a campfire being a person.

The shonen villain test: A great villain is someone the hero could have become. Your player is bonding with relics, transforming, merging with forces beyond themselves. The villain is doing the same thing — just further along, and without the anchor of human connection to slow them down. They looked at the broken world and said "I'll fix it" and never asked if anyone wanted their version of "fixed."

Expect twists. Expect layers. Expect moments where the villain says something and you think "...they're not wrong though." That's what makes it real.

Creatures

Nothing in this world is evil. Creatures attack because they're hungry for connection they can't have. The Divorce severed natural bonds. A Slime engulfs you not from malice — it's trying to merge, trying to return to the matrimony it vaguely remembers. It just doesn't know how without destroying you. The Dragon's fire is the closest thing it has to reaching out.

The more warped a creature is, the more desperately it's trying to reconnect. The most dangerous enemies aren't the angriest — they're the loneliest. The Dark Knight is a person who bonded with armor so completely they forgot there was a person inside. The Skeleton is what remains when the bond between body and soul partially worked but didn't hold.

This reframes combat. You're not killing monsters. You're meeting something that can't speak. Every fight is a failed matrimony — two beings reaching through the only language the broken world left them. When you Soul Bond with a weapon, you're doing what the creatures tried to do. You just succeeded.

People and Belief

People live in small, warm, fragile clusters. A campfire with three people. A ruin with a family in one room. Groups larger than a few dozen feel the Drift — people get restless, forgetful, wander off. Connection at scale doesn't hold. So the world is intimate by necessity.

Individual characters carry their own beliefs about why the world is the way it is. Not factions or religions — personal philosophies shaped by individual experience:

  • Come across one person who thinks God is dreaming and the world is the dream. Sit with that. Your conversations with them will be strange and real.
  • Meet one person who collects fragments and tries to weave them back together, convinced the Divorce can be repaired with enough patience. Watch them fail. Watch them try again.
  • Find one person who stopped hoping for reunion and just lives — shares food, tends a garden, doesn't think about cosmic questions. They're the happiest person you'll meet. That means something.
  • Encounter one person who believes the Divorce was a birth, not a death. Separation is growth. They're terrifyingly optimistic and might be right.

Each is a full character. Each has a name, a history, a reason they believe what they believe. Interact with them and the player forms their own understanding of the world — not from lore dumps, but from people.

The Player

Largely silent protagonist. Exists through action, not dialogue. A relic hunter who started hearing frequencies — feeling the old bonds that most people can't. They didn't ask for this. They're figuring it out as they go.

The journey: finding familiar things in an unfamiliar world. Soul bonding with weapons as a surprise. Meeting creatures — hostile or not — warped by residual energy. Learning to defend yourself without knowing why. The innate human desire for survival. Looking for people. Wanting a friend. Finding one.

The player can't fully learn what caused the Divorce. Like Bastion — you piece together fragments, you deduce, you feel the shape of it. But the full truth is beyond reach. You make the most of what's here. That's enough. That has to be enough.

Evanui

A kid. Goes by Evan at first — just a nickname, just a kid at the campfire. Later you learn the full name: Evanui.

Évanouir — French: to vanish, to disappear, to pass out of existence.

He can't bond with relics. Can't transform. But he doesn't Drift. In a world where connection dissolves, where groups scatter, where people wander off mid-conversation — he stays. He attached himself to you because you're the first person who also stuck around. Maybe the only power that matters in this world isn't bonding with ancient weapons. Maybe it's the ability to remain.

He's funny. Actually funny — the way a real kid is. He rates your weapons out of ten. He has a ranking of every creature you've fought based on "how much it looked like it smelled bad." He says things that are accidentally profound and intentionally gross. He falls asleep during your dramatic weapon awakening. He's living through the quiet end of the world and he thinks your Drain Touch is "nasty but cool."

He's the human thread. The anchor. The proof that some bonds hold without ancient power or cosmic force. Just stubbornness. Just a kid who decided to stay.

What Shonen Gets Right (Design Pillars)

This game should nail everything the shonen genre does at its best:

  • Fights that MEAN something. Not grind encounters — each fight reveals character. The enemy's behavior tells you who they are. Your choices tell you who YOU are.
  • Power that costs something. Every transformation has a tradeoff. Every awakening changes you. Getting stronger isn't free and it isn't always good.
  • Bonds between characters that grow and strain. Evanui isn't a static NPC. The relationship changes. It deepens, it gets complicated, it means more as the stakes rise.
  • A villain you understand. Forced Matrimony makes sense. That's what makes it terrifying. The best shonen villains are mirrors of the hero — same wound, different response.
  • Humor that's real. Not comic relief breaks — humor woven into the texture. People are funny because people are funny, even in dark times. Especially in dark times.
  • Moments of absolute coolness. The first weapon awakening. The first transformation. The first time a combo fires and the screen flashes. These moments should make you want to screenshot and send to a friend.
  • Depth that rewards attention. The surface is fun. The lore is there for those who dig. The combat system has layers that reveal themselves over dozens of hours. You can enjoy it casually or obsess over it — it rewards both.
  • Something absolutely REAL underneath. The loneliness is real. The desire for connection is real. The question of whether wholeness requires losing yourself — that's real. Palatable on the surface, genuine underneath. That's what separates great shonen from disposable shonen.

Open Questions (Workshop Later)

  • Regional conflict structure — something like Cradle vs. Reach? Needs workshopping.
  • Escalation mechanic — Surges? Creature waves tied to bonding destabilization? Promising but TBD.
  • World name — unnamed for now. Maybe stays unnamed.
  • Villain identity — who specifically? What's their history? What did they lose?
  • Secondary cast beyond Evanui — love interest? Rival? Mentor figure?
  • The ending — complete the Matrimony (lose self, become the world) vs. stay human (stay separate, stay with Evanui, accept incompleteness)? Or a third option nobody expected?
