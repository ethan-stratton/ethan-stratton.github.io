## The Little Guy — Deep Design Session (evening, ~05:00-14:00 UTC)

### Philosophical Framework Added
- Ethan shared Robert Moore's "Facing the Dragon" — grandiosity as divine fire present in all creation
- Also Bruce Hafen's "Faith is Not Blind" — Simplicity → Complexity/Confusion → Informed Choice
- **Core thesis:** The little guy's journey IS the player's journey, compressed small enough to see
- Three fractal tiers: Creator (programmer) → Player → Character. Each has fire, each has the same question: "What do I do with this?"
- The game doesn't answer. The player's behavior IS the answer.
- GDD fully rewritten at `little-guy/GAME-DESIGN.md`

### Major New Systems Implemented

#### Mirror System (behavioral tracking)
- Silent tracking: aggression, patience, control, attention
- Guy's AI adapts: aggressive players get flinchy/fearful guy, patient players get curious explorer, controlling players get passive puppet
- Cursor flinch within 80px for aggressive players
- Word generation weighted by behavior scores

#### "How Are You?" Click
- Click (not drag, <5px movement) on guy after he has words
- Response assembled from vocabulary + behavioral state
- 10-second cooldown

#### State Persistence (localStorage)
- Full state saved: scene, words, behavior scores, emotional memory, session count
- Saves on scene transition, every 30s, and beforeunload
- Returning players: "... BACK?" after 5+ min absence, warmth after 3+ sessions

#### Pit Redesign (Scene 4)
- Now OPTIONAL — stepping stones offer safe path requiring player-drawn bridges
- Pushing him in: "NO NO NO", just plain ground below, no reward. Tests the player.
- Drawing safe path: "You chose the long way. That matters more than you think."
- pitPath tracked as 'pushed' | 'drawn'

#### Grandiose Word Split (Scene 5)
