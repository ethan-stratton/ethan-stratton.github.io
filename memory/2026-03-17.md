### Future Game Ideas (noted, not implementing yet)
1. **Roaming Super Boss** — Like Death Gaze from FF6. A boss that moves between levels on the overworld map. You have to hunt it down or it ambushes you mid-transition. Could tie into the Mecha Dragon mystery.
2. **Spawning Grounds** — Enemy types grow stronger the longer you're away from an area. Neglect a cleared zone and it festers. Creates pressure to revisit and maintain your territory. Ties into the civ-building loop naturally.
3. **Cross-Level Enemy Chase** — Some enemies can follow you through level transitions. You can't just run — they'll be waiting on the other side. Creates tension around loading zones.

### Video Reference: "Why RPG Developers Should Ripoff the Original Dragon Quest" (The Crawl)
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7aEmVshZRRc
- Couldn't extract transcript but key DQ1 design principles apply to GENESYS:
  - Elegant simplicity: one town, one dungeon, one enemy at a time
  - World visible but gated by difficulty, not locks
  - Systems introduced organically through necessity, not tutorials
  - Overworld IS the progression
- Aligns with our "no forced pauses" principle and EVE's examine system
