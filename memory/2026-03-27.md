## Seventh Son — New Game Concept — 19:05-21:00 UTC

### Overview
- Ethan's second game idea, captivated him at work. Modernized Link to the Past formula.
- 2D 3/4 perspective, high fantasy, Minish Cap/Four Swords art style
- "Children's game with adult themes" — Dark Crystal, Coraline, Iron Giant tone
- Created `/home/node/.openclaw/workspace/seventh_son/GAME-DESIGN.md`

### Core Premise
- Play as seventh son of seventh son, born with magical sight (glowing eyes)
- Grandfather was king, engineered the prophecy across two generations, now dead
- Father was the one brother who rejected knighthood, became a farmer, fled
- Father has disappeared. Six knight uncles want to steal your eyes via dark magic
- You disguise as a blind monk to hide identity. Fist combat + monk playstyle when disguised.

### Key Design Decisions
- **The "dark world" twist** = true sight vs normal vision. Blindfold off reveals hidden layer of world (corruption, spirits, hidden doors, true forms). Every area has two visual interpretations without needing a separate map.
- **Blind monk mechanic**: blindfold on = safe in towns, fist combat, can't see magical layer. Blindfold off = full power + true sight but hunted (bounty system, glowing eyes visible).
- **Weapon durability** is central. Crystal (high dmg/low dur), iron (balanced/repairable), wood/bone (weak/abundant). Always cycling weapons, falling back to fists.
- **Three enemy types**: natural (melee works), armored (break armor first, magic bypasses), magical (magic-only kills, forces blindfold removal)
- **Death/revival mechanic**: seventh son can revive but each death has a cost (lose memory, world shifts)
- **6 biomes/dungeons**: Crystal Forest, Floating Isle, Dragon's Isle, Drowned March, Iron Citadel, Whispering Vale

### Ethan's Teenage Notes (recovered)
- Word mood boards in Latin/German/English — alchemical + political intrigue vocabulary
- "Internecine" = the thesis (mutually destructive family conflict)
- Original story: uncles are knights, father was farmer, uncles want to rip out your eyes
- Game over screen: statue with bleeding eyes and screaming mouth
- Hidden kill counter drop system, stat system (STR/CON/INT/LCK/DEF/ATK)

### Ethan's Design Values

- Avoid melodramatic dialogue, bland characters, cliche storylines
- Not heavily movement-gated like Genesys — more story/quest/puzzle driven progression
- Needs a clear overarching goal (proposed: find father, save sight, unite kingdom)
