### Scanning Design Research (~00:52 UTC)
**Reddit thread** (r/Metroid) — key community complaints/solutions for Prime scanning:
- Scan time too long — reduce it
- Reading text in menu breaks immersion — use audio readouts (robotic voice, like Jarvis)
- Switching to scan visor makes world ugly — integrate scanning into lock-on, no separate visor
- No feedback on room completion — show indicator for "fully scanned room"
- Better sound/visual feedback for successful scans
- Make scanning optional/customizable for different playstyles

**YouTube video** (CgA6XOuaT7Y) — "Metroid Prime Had A Scan-tastic Story":
- Prime's scan visor IS the storytelling device — it's how 100% of the lore is delivered
- Engagement is player-controlled: you can ignore it entirely (bare-bones action game) or scan everything (rich lore experience)
- Scanning made Samus feel "cerebral and calculating" — intellectual character trait, not just a mechanic
- The lore is ALWAYS tied to what you're currently doing — Chozo Ruins lore = Chozo history, Pirate Labs lore = pirate experiments. Context-sensitive.
- Weakness: "you walk into a room teeming with enemies... then spend 5 minutes staring at computer screens" — pace-breaking
- Strength: unreliable narrators (Chozo are mystical, Pirates are pragmatic) — when they agree, it's definitely true. Creates layered understanding.

**Our design response — how Genesis scanning avoids Prime's pitfalls:**
1. NO separate visor/mode. Scan is contextual — press E near creature, no mode switch
2. Results are GAMEPLAY, not text. Scan level 1 = name, level 2 = HP bar, level 3 = weak point + damage bonus. You never NEED to read anything.
3. EVE delivers audio summary (future: text bubble, 1-2 sentences max). No menu diving.
4. Scan time is 1 second, not 5+. Quick and snappy.
5. Scanning happens IN combat — you can move while scanning. No pace break.
6. Ecosystem knowledge = environmental puzzle solving. The reward for scanning isn't lore, it's new gameplay options.
