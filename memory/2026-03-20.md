### Key Story Decisions
- **Ending B (become the successor/new Dragon) is CANON.** Ending A (stay and build) is the "safe" incomplete ending. Ending C is ultra-hidden teaser for sequel/larger universe
- **Dragon summoning (Beat 24):** If Adam refused EVE's knowledge, he must summon the Dragon (Simon's Quest style). If he accepted, he goes directly + gets a dimensional rift (unique optional zone with Dragon's perspective). Both paths trigger a world event (harder enemies globally)
- **EVE's discovery (Beat 21-22):** She pieces together native creation myths + data core → prophecy describes Adam specifically, predicts a "terrible fate" (becoming the successor). This is the Temptation/Fruit — player can accept or refuse
- **Altar quest (Beat 14):** Natives ask Adam to perform a biblical act; during it, technology is INSTANTLY drained — reveals planet actively suppresses tech (not passive corrosion)
- **Natives intentionally vague** — conflicting creation myths between settlements, players theory-craft, no confirmed origin
- **Guardian (Beat 8):** Mythological creature from Bible or world religion creation myths (Mushushu from Babylon suggested). TBD
- **Dragon dialogue arc:** Silent (prologue) → terrifying/cryptic (Act 2) → articulate/melancholy (Act 3-4). Dragon doesn't change; Adam's ability to understand it grows
- **Beat 11 world opens with no direction** — deliberate, encourages exploration. Hybrid tech acquisition = story progression threshold
- **"Rewarding the action you're already doing"** — SOTN philosophy. Exploration IS rewarded, not grind gates (Bloodstained anti-pattern)
