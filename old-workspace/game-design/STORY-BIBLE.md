# Story Bible — [Working Title TBD]

## Logline
A space hero crash-lands on a foreign planet after losing a battle with a cosmic evil. Stranded and stripped of everything, he must fight through hostile territory, build civilization from nothing, and discover that his "fall" was actually his salvation.

## Thematic Core
**What looks like the Fall is actually the path to becoming whole.**

The story is a sci-fi retelling of Genesis, inspired by LDS scriptural context where the Fall isn't punishment — it's progression. Eve's choice wasn't a curse, it was the bravest decision in the story.

### Central Question
"Was losing everything the only way to become something more?"

---

## Characters

### ADAM (Player Character)
- Elite pilot/soldier for the Galactic Federation
- Competent, dutiful, maybe a little hollow — he's good at his job but hasn't been tested in a way that matters
- Starts the game completing a mission, heading home
- The crash strips him of rank, equipment, identity. He has to rebuild from nothing.
- His arc: duty → blame → understanding → gratitude → transformation

### EVE (AI Companion)
- The ship's AI assistant — your partner throughout the game
- Makes the split-second decision to crash-land on the planet when the fight with the Dragon is lost
- Player's initial reaction may be to blame her ("you crashed us here")
- The truth: she saved your life. The crash was the only survivable option.
- She can't fight, but she can analyze, suggest, remember, and guide
- Helps with whichever gameplay pillar the player struggles with
- Her arc: tool → scapegoat → partner → equal → something more
- **Meta layer:** the player is literally building this game with an AI. EVE mirrors that.

### THE MECHA DRAGON (Satan / Antagonist)
- Cybernetic space dragon — ancient, intelligent, cosmic-scale threat
- Attacks Adam's ship on the way home. Cryptic dialogue that only makes sense later.
- Possible motivation: doesn't want Adam to reach home, OR doesn't want Adam to become what the planet will make him
- Hunts Adam throughout the game — appears as boss encounters, sends minions
- The dragon's followers infiltrate the civilization Adam builds

### NPCs (Genesis Archetypes)
- **Cain analog** — a settler who becomes jealous, violent. Kills his brother. Player must decide how to handle it.
- **Abel analog** — innocent, productive, beloved. His death is a gut punch.
- **Dragon worshippers** — NPCs who secretly serve the Mecha Dragon. Sabotage, betrayal, false religion vs genuine faith.
- **Builders, farmers, leaders** — people who join your civilization and have their own stories
- **Prophetic figures** — NPCs who hint at the bigger picture, speak in scripture-like riddles

## Story Structure

### Prologue (Playable — SotN Richter-style)
- Adam is in his ship, finishing a mission, heading home
- The Mecha Dragon attacks. You fight it in a ship combat / action sequence
- You lose. Cryptic dialogue from the Dragon: lines that don't make sense yet
- EVE makes the call — crashes the ship on a nearby planet
- Smash cut. Title screen.

### Act 1: The Fall
- Wake up on the planet. Ship destroyed. Equipment gone.
- EVE is partially functional — limited help at first, grows with you
- Learn basic movement, combat with improvised weapons
- First hostile area to clear. First piece of land to settle.
- Initial tone: isolation, survival, resentment ("why did you crash us here?")

### Act 2: Genesis
- Building civilization. Clearing more land. Meeting NPCs.
- Cain/Abel storyline plays out. Player choices matter.
- Dragon worshippers emerge — mistrust among your people
- EVE becomes more capable and more of a partner
- New movement abilities and weapons unlock
- The planet starts to feel like home

### Act 3: Revelation
- The player realizes the Fall was necessary
- The Dragon returns for a major confrontation
- The civilization you built is threatened
- Adam isn't the same person who crashed — he's more
- EVE reveals something about herself or the planet that reframes everything

### Finale
- Final battle with the Mecha Dragon
- Resolution of the "getting home" question — does home mean what it used to?
- Adam is transformed. The Fall was the point.

## Themes

1. **The Fall as progression, not punishment** — LDS reading of Genesis
2. **Blame vs gratitude** — EVE made the hard choice. When do you realize that?
3. **Building meaning from nothing** — civilization as therapy, creation as identity
4. **False worship** — the Dragon's followers vs genuine faith/purpose
5. **What is home?** — the Federation is "heaven" but is going back really the goal?
6. **Transformation through suffering** — you have to lose everything to become whole
